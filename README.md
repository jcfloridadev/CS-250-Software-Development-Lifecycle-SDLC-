## Reflection

### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How do user stories help?

In my experience, interpreting user needs starts with understanding what the user is trying to achieve and translating that into clear, actionable requirements. User stories help simplify this process by framing requirements from the user’s perspective. During the SNHU Travel project, user stories allowed me to break down features into smaller, manageable tasks that could be prioritized and implemented step by step. This made development more organized and ensured that the final product aligned with user expectations.

### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I plan to use?

I approach developing programs using an iterative and incremental mindset. Instead of trying to complete everything at once, I focus on building functionality in smaller parts and continuously improving it. Agile processes such as sprint planning, daily stand-ups, and retrospectives helped keep the project organized and adaptable. In the future, I plan to continue using these Agile practices to improve efficiency, track progress, and quickly respond to changes or feedback.

### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, being accountable for assigned tasks, and supporting the overall goals of the team. It also involves being open to feedback and willing to adapt when challenges arise. During this project, collaboration and communication played a key role in ensuring that all components worked together effectively. A strong team member contributes not only through technical work but also by maintaining a positive and cooperative attitude.
